4. Basic First-Aid Guide

Choking

For Adults & Children over 1 year:

  • Encourage the person to cough.
  • If coughing doesn't work, give 5 sharp back blows between their shoulder blades.
  • Follow with 5 abdominal thrusts (Heimlich maneuver).
  • Call for emergency help immediately.

Minor Burns

Treatment:

  • Immediately cool the burn under cool or lukewarm running water for at least 10-20 minutes.
  • Do not use ice, iced water, or greasy substances like butter.
  • Remove any jewelry or clothing from the affected area before it swells.
  • Cover with a sterile, non-fluffy dressing.

Allergic Reactions

Signs of severe reaction (anaphylaxis):

  • Difficulty breathing or wheezing.
  • Swelling of the throat, tongue, or lips.
  • Faintness or dizziness.

Action:

  • Call for emergency help immediately.
  • If the person has an adrenaline auto-injector (e.g., EpiPen), help them use it.
  • Lie them down and raise their legs.

Cuts & Scrapes

Treatment for minor cuts:

  • Wash your hands thoroughly before treating the wound.
  • Gently wash the wound with clean water and mild soap.
  • Apply gentle pressure with a clean cloth or gauze to stop any bleeding.
  • Apply an antiseptic ointment and cover the wound with a clean bandage.

Fainting (Syncope)

When someone faints:

  • Lay the person down and elevate their legs above heart level.
  • Loosen tight clothing, belts, or collars.
  • Make sure they have fresh air.
  • If they don't regain consciousness in a minute, call for emergency help.
